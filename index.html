Smart Aquaculture & Food Security: Oxygen Depletion Rates

Field Application: Sustainable Food Security (SFM) — Intensive Tilapia Farming in Malaysia.

## The Bioscience Problem

In intensive Malaysian aquaculture systems (e.g., mechanised ponds in Hulu Langat or Perak), maintaining Dissolved Oxygen (DO) levels above 3.0 mg/L is vital for Tilapia survival. If automated aerators fail, biomass respiration consumes oxygen rapidly.

Instead of knowing just *how much* oxygen is left, farm management systems must calculate the instantaneous rate of depletion ($f'(x)$) to forecast exactly when the fish will hit critical hypoxia stress, allowing predictive AI triggers to deploy backup oxygen.

### Oxygen Level Function: $f(x)$

Models the DO level (mg/L) over time $x$ (hours) post-aerator failure:

f(x) = 0.05x³ - 0.6x² + 7.5

*Initial DO at failure (x=0) is 7.5 mg/L.

### Rate of Depletion: $f'(x)$

The first derivative yields the instantaneous rate of change (mg/L per hour):

f'(x) = 0.15x² - 1.2x

*Calculated using the Power Rule in calculus.

Data Baseline Reference: Parameters calibrated based on real-world aquaculture thresholds found in the "Department of Fisheries Malaysia (DOF) Aquaculture Water Quality Standards" and "Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute (MARDI) Tilapia Growth Performance Indexes".
